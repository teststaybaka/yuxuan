A Danmaku video website

What is Danmaku? It means comments that can fly across the screen while you are watching videos. As far as I know, it's originated in Japan. The most popular Dankau video website in Japan is Niconico, which is launched in 2006. Several video websites in China followed up, such as AcFun, Bilibili. Danmaku became more and more popular that almost every video wbsite in China implements this core function. What surprised me is that after so many years, YouTube never tried to incorporate Danmaku function. Since I'm not a YouTube developer, I'm not able to implement this function on it. Then here comes the idea: Why not create an English version of Niconico? You may say that Niconico has English version. To be honest, the UI design of Niconico is not that friendly. Moreover, the Danmaku function will be implemented in HTML5 instead of Flash Player. As what Niconico did before, I'm going to use YouTube as a video source. Now, this idea is launched as a project called DanTube (the combination of Danmaku and YouTube). Check out its progress here. I'm now building the website from scratch with Google App Engine and webapp2. All basic functions are being implemented and more features will be added in the future.

Online movies, series and anime purchase service

This idea is not thoroughly investigated. What I'm thinking is to add more features to DanTube. Visitors can purchase/sell videos, like movies, online. Two things make my idea different from the other video purchase/selling site: 1. Even movies are treated as usual videos with danmaku and comments once purchased; 2. Price is related with resolution. You may not understand what does the second thing mean. It's not saying that if your video has low resolution, you can't sell it for a high price. It means that your video will be sold with different prices with respect to different resolutions. Any customers who wants to purchase your video need to choose in which resolution he would like to watch. Why would one hope to watch a low resolution version? Chances are he may think that the video he is going to watch doesn't deserve its price, while he just want to have a try or kill time. You can think of other possible user stories but I'm sure it will eventually benefit all video creators.

Action game of Touhou Project

I, as a fan of action games, especially DMC3 and DMC4, always hope to create my own game some day. You won't make any difference, if you just hope. So I decided to start developing it in June, 2014. So many techinque problems lie in my way, modeling, texture, motion and lots of design tasks but I won't be daunted. The very fisrt problem is what the story is of the game and what characters look like. After some consideration, I found that Touhou Project is a good start. Its creator, ZUN, supports second creation and it's widely known over the world. One of its characters, Youmu who fights with two swords is suitable for action game. Therefore I decided to name the game as Youmu Blade Dance. It's developed with Maya and Unity3D. Although I'm saything that I'm developing a game, it's actually learning, studying and developing at the same time. I'm just a beginner of game development, so the progress is quite slow. It's hard to say how much I've completed in the past several months but I did learn a lot.
